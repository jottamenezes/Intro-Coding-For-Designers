One of the first things that you will notice when you start to code in any language, is that you will be the granter of names. Parts of code need to be gifted a name to exist and function. Some words in programming are given to you by the people who created the language, other words you decide yourself. What purpose will the name you give hold? Are you naming something so that it is easy to understand amongst peers who will be working with it? Is it purely efficient as to not be too long length wise? Poetry? Humor?

Lets share the story of how we got our names.

Always Already Programming

By Melanie Hoff

Everyone who interacts with computers has in important ways always already been programming them.

Every time you make a folder or rename a file on your computer, the actions you take through moving your mouse and clicking on buttons, translate into text-based commands or scripts which eventually translate into binary.
