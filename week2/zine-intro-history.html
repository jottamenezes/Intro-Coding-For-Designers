Zines, also known as fanzines or mini-mags, are self-published and self-distributed print publications that emerged in the late 1970s and early 1980s.

They are usually created with a photocopier or other low-cost printing methods and are often produced in small quantities and distributed through mail networks, independent bookstores, and other alternative channels.

Commonlly produced by marginalized or underground communities who wanted to create and distribute their own content outside of mainstream media channels
